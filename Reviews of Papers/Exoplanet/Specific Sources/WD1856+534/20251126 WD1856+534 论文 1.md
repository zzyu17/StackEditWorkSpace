### 1 Core Information Summary

*   **Research Question:** Does the WD 1856+534 b system show evidence of orbital evolution (period change) or the presence of additional planets through transit timing variations (TTVs), in order to constrain its formation history and tidal properties?
    
*   **Methodology:** Used new transit observations (20 from the Lick Nickel telescope) combined with literature data (48 transits) to extend the timing baseline. Applied MCMC fits to constant-period and orbital-decay/growth models, and used the TTVFast code to perform grid searches and MCMC simulations for hypothetical companion planets.
    
*   **Core Findings:**
    
    1.  No evidence for orbital decay or growth was found. The period derivative is consistent with zero:  $\dot{P} = 0.04 \pm 0.43 \, \text{ms yr}^{-1}$ .
        
    2.  A lower limit on the planetary tidal quality factor was set at  $Q'_{p} \gtrsim 3.1 \times 10^{6}$ , similar to Jupiter's. An upper limit on orbital eccentricity was set at  $e \lesssim 10^{-2}$ .
        
    3.  Ruled out the presence of an additional planet with a mass  $> 4.1 \, M_{J}$  and orbital period  $< 1500$  days.
        
*   **Stated Limitations/Future Work:** The study cannot rule out smaller close-in planets or larger planets at greater distances as potential perturbers. Continued transit timing over an extended baseline is justified to further probe the system's formation.
